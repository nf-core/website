The nf-core community moves fast.
In a normal month there are a handful of pipelines with new releases, one or two typically ship their very first release, there are new blog posts, bytesize talks, training events, hackathons and all sorts.
If you don't follow Slack and the website every day, it's easy to lose track of all of it.

So, we built a newsletter. Once a month it gathers what happened across nf-core onto a single page, and you can have that page emailed to you.
Sign up below:

You can also sign up to read it [on LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/newsletters/nf-core-7474718981564706816/).

The whole thing is rendered on the website, so you can read the latest edition and browse previous months at [nf-co.re/newsletter](/newsletter).

## What's in an edition

Each edition is assembled from the website, so it matches what's already published:

- New blog posts
- Security advisories
- Pipeline releases, with brand-new pipelines' first releases highlighted
- Upcoming bytesize talks, trainings, and hackathons
- Newly minted pipeline repos, plus pipeline and RFC proposals from [nf-core/proposals](https://github.com/nf-core/proposals/issues)

There's also an "in case you missed it" section that shows blogs and other things from the past couple of months, in case something slipped past you the first time.

## Read it however suits you

If you're viewing the newsletter on the website, you'll find a few links at the top of each edition which give you the same content in different shapes:

- The newsletter in a regular web page
- The email-friendly HTML version
- Markdown, if you'd rather read it in a terminal or hand it to an LLM
- A stripped-back plain HTML version (useful for copying + pasting)
- An [RSS feed](/newsletter/rss.xml) you can follow in a reader

## Get it by email

The emails go out on the first Wednesday of every month. Sign up here:

<NewsletterSignup compact={true} />

A few details:

- Signing up is double opt-in. You enter your address, we email you a confirmation link, and you join the list only once you click it.
- We keep your email address and the date you subscribed, and nothing else. Sending runs on [Amazon SES](https://aws.amazon.com/ses/) in the EU (`eu-west-1`).
- Every email carries an in-body unsubscribe link and the standard `List-Unsubscribe` headers, so Gmail, Apple Mail and similar clients show their own one-click unsubscribe button next to the sender. Leaving the list takes a single click, wherever you read your mail.

## Get it on LinkedIn

We know that people's inboxes are already full, so if you prefer you can also get the newsletter [on LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/newsletters/nf-core-7474718981564706816/).

We'll do our best to publish it there each month (it's impossible to automate, so some clicking is required), but it'll be the same content in both places.
